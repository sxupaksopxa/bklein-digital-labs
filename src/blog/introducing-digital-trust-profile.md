## Introducing Digital Trust Profile

*Published: July 2026*

### Why another trust standard?

Artificial intelligence is changing the way people interact with digital services. Today we can communicate not only with websites and applications, but also with AI assistants, autonomous agents, and systems capable of making decisions on behalf of organizations.
As these systems become more autonomous, an important question emerges:
**How can we determine whether we should trust them?**

Today, users often have little information beyond a company logo or a privacy policy. There is no simple, standardized way to understand who operates a digital service, whether AI is involved, or how decisions are supervised.
Digital Trust Profile (DTP) is a proposal to address this gap.

### The idea

Digital Trust Profile is a machine-readable trust profile that digital services can publish to describe themselves.
Rather than replacing existing security standards, DTP complements them by providing transparent information that both humans and software can understand.
A Digital Trust Profile may include information such as:
- Organization operating the service
- Responsible contact
- Verification status
- AI usage disclosure
- Human oversight
- Jurisdiction
- Privacy information
- Security certifications
- Version information

The objective is not to calculate trust automatically, but to provide structured information that enables informed trust decisions.

### Why now?

The internet was built around human users.
The next generation of digital services will increasingly involve AI systems communicating with other AI systems.
Autonomous agents may schedule meetings, negotiate contracts, purchase services, or exchange information without direct human interaction.
For these interactions, transparency becomes essential.
Digital Trust Profile aims to provide a common language for describing digital trust.

### Long-term vision

The long-term vision is to establish an open standard that can be adopted by organizations, software vendors, and public institutions.
Potential applications include:
- Enterprise systems
- AI assistants
- Autonomous agents
- Browser extensions
- Procurement platforms
- Governance tools
- Security platforms

By publishing a Digital Trust Profile, organizations can make it easier for both people and automated systems to understand who they are and how their services operate.

### Current Status

Digital Trust Profile is an active open-source project.
The initial specification has been published on GitHub and a first reference implementation has already been integrated into the AI Governance Model Lab, where Digital Trust Profiles can be created and validated as part of AI governance assessments.

- 📄 **Project repository:** https://github.com/sxupaksopxa/digital-trust-profile
- 🌐 **Live demonstration:** https://ai-governance-model-lab.vercel.app/digital-trust

The project is currently focused on refining the specification, collecting community feedback, and exploring practical use cases for broader adoption.
Current deliverables include:
- Initial specification
- JSON schema
- Reference implementation
- Example Digital Trust Profiles
- Documentation
- Integration with the AI Governance Model Lab

Future work includes:
- Expanding the specification
- Developing additional validation tools
- Building interoperability examples
- Encouraging community discussion and contributions

### Closing thoughts

Trust cannot be generated automatically.
However, transparency can.
Digital Trust Profile is an attempt to make transparency easier to publish, easier to consume, and easier to verify in an increasingly AI-driven digital world.
I look forward to discussing the idea with developers, architects, governance professionals, and anyone interested in improving trust in digital interactions.
